Interested in what we are testing? See below for more information.

We are a bunch of scientists exploring human aesthetic experiences and their relationship with the format of mental representations. In particular, this study looks into a phenomenon called the prototype preference, where people find typical members of a category more visually pleasing than atypical members of the same category.

Recently, an alternative explanation was proposed that made a unique prediction: Human prefers typical members because their representative features allow us to access category-related information easier. At the same time, most of the categories in the world that we frequently interact with and tested in the experiments are positive or at least neutral. Thus, the typicality of a category member facilitate access to more positive information. This idea makes an unusual prediction: The prototype preference should end up being the prototype aversion when a negative category is under consideration instead!

That is what we are testing here with all those insects, guns, and snakes that are usually considered negative. We will find out soon if people indeed dislike typical insects, guns, or snakes more than atypical ones.
